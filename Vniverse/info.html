Vniverse [1.0]

Stephanie Strickland and Ian Hatcher

vniverse.com

Vniverse is a poetry instrument you can play.

In DRAW mode, touch and drag to create your own constellations.

In CONSTELLATIONS mode, explore the ten constellations appearing in the coordinate print book of poems, V : WaveTercets / Losing L’una (SpringGun Press, 2014).

WAVETERCETS mode plays the entire poem for you, starting at the beginning. Or, by touching any star, you may begin anywhere you like.

ORACLE mode allows you to pose seven questions to the sky.

The CLEAR button clears the sky.

Stephanie Strickland’s V first appeared in print from Penguin, 2002, as an invertible book with two beginnings: V : WaveSon.nets / Losing L’una, winner of the Poetry Society’s Di Castagnola Award. Online it has two additional parts: V : Vniverse (in collaboration with Cynthia Lawson Jaramillo) and Errand Upon Which We Came (in collaboration with M.D. Coverley). It appears in the Electronic Literature Collection 2 (2011).

In 2014, V appears in a new print edition from SpringGun Press with a new title, V : WaveTercets / Losing L’una, a new rendering, and this app, created in collaboration with Ian Hatcher.

Stephanie Strickland has written 7 books of print poetry, including Dragon Logic, Ahsahta Press, 2013. She has also collaborated on 9 electronic poems, most recently Sea and Spar Between and Duels—Duets with Nick Montfort and House of Trust with Ian Hatcher. A member of the Board of Directors of the Electronic Literature Organization, she co-edited Electronic Literature Collection 1. stephaniestrickland.com

Ian Hatcher is a text/sound/performance artist, musician, and programmer. Another poetry book/app project, Abra, co-created with Amaranth Borsuk and Kate Durbin and supported by the Center for Book and Paper Arts, will appear in spring of 2014. ianhatcher.net
